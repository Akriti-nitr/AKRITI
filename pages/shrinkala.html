  • POP ART
    • Sushree Satarupa
    • ART

    Pop-art : Popular, Transient, Expendable, Low cost, Mass produced, Young, Witty, Sexy, Gimmicky, Glamorous, Big business. Pop Art began in the 1950s, became very popular in the 1960s, it started in the United Kingdom, but became a true art movement in New York City. The Pop art movement aimed to solidify the idea that art can draw from any source, and there is no hierarchy of culture to disrupt this. The dots in this art you see are the "Ben-Day dots". Characterized by Recognizable imagery, Bright colors, Irony and Satire ; pop art blur the boundaries between "high" art and "low" culture.

  • PATTACHITRA
    • Dibyansika Biswal
    • ART

    Odisha’s international pride, Pattachitra was born in the 12th century AD. Known for its intricate detailing, Pattachitra painting is a powerful medium to portray folktales and mythological narratives. The name Pattachitra has evolved from the Sanskrit words patta, meaning canvas, and chitra, meaning picture. Pattachitra is created on a canvas died with tamarind pulp. Natural shades are used such as 'Hingula' for red. 'Haritala' for yellow and 'Ramaraja' for blue . The paintings are richly adorned with vibrant motifs and have a customary beautify floral boundary. All the paintings speak out volumes on the tradition and culture of its birthplace. This artform has been accoladed by the government of India and indigenous artists are being encouraged.

  • CUBISIM
    • Muskan
    • ART

    Cubism- unconventional, beyond ordinary, artistic, un-realistic, non-representational, illusional, modern, most celebrated Born due to the noble efforts of Pablo Picasso in 1907, Cubism is one of the most celebrated art forms today. It has an abstract and unconventional touch to it. The sketches are usually parted into different regions to give a 2D plane effect of a 3D view. This art form is mostly devoid of perspectives and realistic figures. By breaking objects and figures down into distinct areas – or planes – the artists aims to show different viewpoints at the same time and within the same space. It is definitely a new taste for the millennials. This art form has inspired millions for years to work beyond the definition and paint their heart.

  • IMPRESSION ART
    • Jogeswar
    • ART

    "Whisky's good proofing water. Tells you who's real and who isn't." - Tommy Shelby. Presenting the astounding artist through an elegant artistry, 'Impressionism'. Impressionism, a radical art movement, coalesced in the 1860s by a group of Paris - based artists, driven by a desire to create works that reflected the world they lived in. Characterised by relatively small, thin, yet visible brush strokes, open composition, inclusion of movement as a crucial element of human perception and experience, and unusual visual angles, impressionism lays emphasis on the practice of plein air painting, or painting outside. Initially derided by critics, this elegant artistry has since been embraced as one of the most popular and influential art styles in western history.

  • POINTILLISM
    • Rajesh
    • ART

    "All birds find shelter during a rain. But Eagle avoids rain by flying above the clouds" - Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Presenting the legend of all times in the form of a unique artistry, 'Pointillism'. Pointillism, developed in 1886 by George Seurat and his student, Paul Signac, the espousers of Neo- Impressionism, is a technique of painting in which small, distinct dots of colour are blended together to form an image. The technique relies on the ability of the eye and mind of the viewer to blend the colour spots into a full range of tones. Also known as chromo-luminarism, it is widely used in the decoration of metallic arms, armour and hand- finished firearms. Once coined by art critics in late 1880s as a mocking connotation to ridicule the works of artists, pointillism evolved to be one of the finest and phenomenal forms of art.

  • EXPRESSIONISM
    • Satyajeet
    • ART

    I paint, not the things I see but the feelings they arouse in me. An artform depicting expressions in its sublime and overemphasised form, Expressionism began to rise to prominence in the 20th century. Rather than dealing with the objective and physical reality, it portrays expression-laden features to invoke emotions and ideas. It is generally developed by inserting and adding elements of excessive exaggeration, fantasy or by emulating or recreating primitive experiences. Vibrant and dynamic in its appearance, it often ventures into dramatic themes to reveal with intensity the sensation of fear, terror and trepidation, often with a grotesque touch. An avant guard style that emerged before World War I, it now has its ubiquitous existence in literature, film, drama and music.

  • INK ART
    • Abismruta Sasamal
    • ART

    'The creatures I gave shelter in my lap, Little did I know, were monsters until I was murdered in my nap, From designing castles on my sore scars, To suppressing my screams with honking cars, They ripped off my belly, burning me with curse, The same monsters now seek survival in alternate universe.' Presenting you a self speaking artistry, Ink art. Ink as a sophisticated artistic medium developed between the 7th and 8th centuries in China, where a range of subjects from religion, history and daily life were illustrated using brushes fashioned from animal hair and feathers. A linear method of making images, ink art is the artist's wish to supplement his outlines with tonal suggestions of three-dimensional form, modelling effected by close juxtaposition of a series of strokes forming areas of hatching or cross-hatching. Because of its permanence, ink drawings demand practice and perfection.

  • RAJPUT PAINT
    • Manasa Deepika
    • ART

    Known for its royalty and nobility in the era of Monarchy, Rajput Paintings hold a prime eminence in the heart of art till date. Having its roots in the royal courts of Rajputana in northern India, it only flourished and evolved with time. It is one of those handful art styles that has an unparalleled appeal among the diversity of masses. Also known as Rajasthani Painting, it is popular for its radiance and multiple hues, for its depiction of expressions in the most sublime form. It largely depicts episodes of epics, palaces and life in general. It surpasses the boundary of canvas and has its engraved existence on the roofs, walls and chambers of palaces and forts that never fail to captivate the the sight of onlookers.

  • WARLI ART
    • Kritarth
    • ART

    Quaint and unique in its aspect, Warli Painting also known as Warli Art is an age-old tribal art that has its roots in Maharashtra. Depicting mother nature in various shades, Warli Art remains untouched by the contemporary style of life and living. The focal points in Warli Art portray the livelihood of primitive tribes. It is composed of beautiful geometric and abstract patterns of various everyday activities pervasive among the tribes like hunting and other rituals. It also showcases the exquisite motifs of flowers and other gems of mother nature. Warli Art is one of the few molds which hasn't been transformed and tarnished by any modish interference and has been successful in keeping its essence alive.
